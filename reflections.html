Chapter 1, 2, & 3 Reflection

The first three chapters of “The Psychology of a Web Developer” show how language, technology, and identity can affect people’s learning processes. The narrator’s journey shows how media shaped her communication and goals. Spleak started as something to comfort her. After further interactions she learned that it wasn't actually as good as she first thought. This defeat made her think about the authenticity and connection of Spleak. This change inspired the narrator’s curiosity about web development and coding, turning frustration into productive creativity. The chapters reflect a story of a growth mindset, where digital interaction becomes a gateway to self-discovery and ambition for the future.

Chapter 4 & 5 Reflection

Chapters 4 and 5 explain how both presentation and real‑time websites shape what users see and how they think. Presentation sites rely on strict templates that filter information, while real‑time sites add interaction that feels freeing but still operates within tight constraints. The chapters show how the tech industry’s pressure and demand for market‑friendly design and simple websites develop shallow engagement and turn people into managers of information instead of critical thinkers.

Chapter 6, 7, & 8

Chapters 6, 7, and 8 continue to show the more personal and emotional side of working in tech, especially how it affects identity and mental health. The chapters contain extensive details about the author's personal life experiences which include his time at coding school and his struggle to handle academic stress because he felt that success needed to be achieved within a time limit, which created an environment that prioritized financial gain over genuine education. The section which describes how users must simply click through agreements without questioning them shows the control that technology exerts over people because they fail to understand its effects on their behavior. The last chapter shows how media consumption leads to information overload which develops into an obsessive disorder that results in harmful consequences for people because it negatively affects their self-perception. The chapters showed me that technology shapes our personal identity while determining our actions. This technology leads to mental health problems when we fail to control our consumption.

CSS Reflection

I found CSS to be both fascinating and majorly frustrating during my first try because this was my first opportunity to design a website through CSS. The process of changing colors and fonts and layouts seemed straightforward at first but became difficult when the results did not match my expectations. The main difficulty I faced involved learning how various properties interact with each other because any slight adjustment would break the entire system. I had difficulty remembering the proper syntax which made it hard for me to understand why my code failed to operate correctly especially with flexbox and element alignment tasks. CSS studies taught me that website design needs extensive time and problem-solving skills but I found satisfaction when my programming work became visible on my display screen.
